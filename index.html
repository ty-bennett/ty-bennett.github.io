Problem Statement

There is currently no central lost and found system, making it difficult for students to report, track, and recover lost items.

There is always a number of students losing items on campus. If we had a central way to report lost items on campus, where they were found, as well as a way to recover those items, students would stop flooding the community Snapchat story with lost Carolina Cards, sets of keys, wallets, or even credit cards.

Affinity Diagram

Affinity Diagram's are a way to collect ideas about a certian topic and group those ideas based on categories. My group and I came up with some ideas about an app that we would make regarding a lost and found system for USC's campus due to there currently being no central way to report, track, and retrieve lost items on campus. Some of these idea categories included how we would advertise our app, what tech stack we would use, how we would collect and apply user's reviews, some basic features that our app would have, and plenty of other ideas.

Sketches

Wireframe sketches are a great way to develop a prototypical view of an app that you are designing. My group and I decided to create three different scenarios for three different kind of users using our app. One scenario is a user who lost an item and is on the forum trying to recover it, the second scenario is a user who found a lost item and is wanting to report it on the forum, and the third scenario is a data scientist who wants to collect different metrics about the most common places items are lost, what kinds of items are lost the most, and how many items have been recovered, lost, or are in the process of recovery now. The data scientist can export all of this data into a usable format, e.g. a .csv file for data processing and metrics in case they would want to visualize this data for presentation.
